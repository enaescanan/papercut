Hello, Student!

Here's a list of a few simple things you can do to lessen your paper consumption and water footprint in the classroom.

Let's be more conscious of our paper consumption!

Tips

#1 Ask

Ask your professors if they’re fine with submissions printed duplex
(double-sided) or printed on recycled paper.

Because as it turns out most professors actually don’t mind!

#2 Check the Label

Vote for sustainable paper products with your purchases!

Choose eco-friendly! Look for PEFC Certified or FSC Certified products. Products with these certification mean they are sustainably sourced and produced. They may be a little pricier but by purchasing them, we vote for sustainable products and for companies that take care of the environment.



#3 Maximize Your Paper

For Printing

For your own documents—readings or digital notes—try printing multiple pages per sheet or duplex to maximize each paper!

  • Printing Duplex and Multiple Pages Per Sheet

For Notes

Create your own stash of reusable paper! The clean side of misprints and dreaded old readings are just as good for notes and to-do-lists!


#4 Collect and Donate

Once you’ve maximized your paper, don’t throw them away! Instead, you can collect and donate them to paper drives. Drop them in the school’s dry paper bin!

Clean and dry paper disposed of in the dry paper bin are stored in the school’s MRF and sold. Once sold, the money collected goes to the maintenance staff’s bonus fund, which they split at the end of the year.

Paper is an integral part of university; there’s not much we can do about that. But, we can be more conscious of our paper consumption.

Choose paper that is sustainably sourced and produced. Consume only the paper you need, making sure to maximize each one. And lastly, dispose of your paper properly.
